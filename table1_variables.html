Table 1. Morphological, ecological, hydrological, and landscape context variables used for data collection

This table includes their definition, measurement method, and data type.

Category Variables Definition Method Data type
Morphology Bank type NVO subtype classification (e.g., gently sloping, shallow water bench, wet bank, and forebank) Visual classification Categorical
Bank profile type Bank morphology is classified as gentle slope, steep slope, or stepped profile Field-based visual classification Categorical
Bank slope Slope ratio measured as horizontal distance (m) to vertical height difference (m) Tape measure, marked stick Continuous (ratio); NA for other profiles
Substrate thickness in the aquatic and amphibian zone Thickness of the soft sediment layer at the bottom of the channel Marked rake (penetration difference method) Continuous (cm)
Water depth Maximum water depth in the amphibious and aquatic zones Marked rake at the zone boundary and channel centre Continuous (cm)
Water width Distance between the two banks at the water surface Tape measure, measuring from water line to water line across the channel Continuous (m)
Aquatic zone width Width of the permanently submerged part of the waterway Tape measure Continuous (m)
Amphibian zone width Width of the shallow transition zone between water and land Tape measure Continuous (m)
Morphology Terrestrial zone width Width of the vegetated zone above the high-water line Tape measure Continuous (m)
Landscape context Surrounding land use Classified as agricultural pasture, unimproved grassland, or semi-natural Visual field classification Categorical
Ecology Amphibian species occurrence Unique emergent macrophyte species were recorded in each 1 m x 1 m quadrat replicate at each perpendicular transect in the amphibian zone. 1m x 1m quadrat (n=1 replicates per location) Categorical (species names)
Aquatic macrophyte species occurrence Unique macrophyte species recorded from rake-toss and dipnet-sweep replicates within the aquatic zone. Rake toss (n= 3 replicates per location) and dipnet sweep (n=3 replicates per location); species recorded per replicate and summed to determine total richness per location Categorical (species names)
Hydrology Light penetration Depth at which the Secchi disc is no longer visible Secchi disc attached to marked line Continuous (cm)
Bottom visibility Visual assessment before physically disturbing the bottom substrate. Visual observation from shore Binary (FALSE/TRUE)
Total nitrogen (N) Dissolved total nitrogen in the water column 50 mL water sample, laboratory analysis Continuous (mg/L)
Total phosphorus (P) Dissolved phosphorus concentration in the water column 50 mL water sample, laboratory analysis Continuous (mg/L)
Electrical conductivity (EC) Measure of the ability to conduct an electrical current 50 mL water sample, laboratory analysis Continuous (mS/cm)

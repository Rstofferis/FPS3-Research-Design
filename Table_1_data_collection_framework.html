Table 3. Data Collection Framework

Morphological, ecological, hydrological, and landscape context variables collected across 13 NVO sites in the Hoogheemraadschap van Delfland management area, including their definition, measurement method, and data type.

Variable Definition Method Data type
Morphology
Bank type NVO subtype classification (e.g., gently sloping, shallow water bench, wet bank, and forebank) Visual classification Categorical
Bank profile type Bank morphology classified as gentle slope, steep slope, or stepped profile Field-based visual classification Categorical
Bank slope Slope ratio measured as horizontal distance to vertical height difference for gentle slopes only Tape measure, marked stick Continuous (ratio)
Substrate thickness Thickness of the soft sediment layer at the bottom of the channel, measured in aquatic and amphibian zones Marked rake (penetration difference method) Continuous (cm)
Water depth Maximum water depth in the amphibious and aquatic zones Marked rake at zone boundary and channel centre Continuous (cm)
Channel width Distance between both banks at the water surface Google Earth Pro and field verification with tape measure Continuous (m)
Aquatic zone width Width of the permanently submerged part of the waterway, from the end of one amphibian zone to the start of the other Tape measure Continuous (m)
Amphibian zone width Width of the shallow transition zone between water and land Tape measure Continuous (m)
Terrestrial zone width Width of the vegetated zone above the high-water line Tape measure Continuous (m)
Landscape Context
Surrounding land use Dominant land use type within the area around the NVO: agricultural pasture, unimproved grassland, or semi-natural Visual field classification Categorical
Ecology
Amphibian vegetation occurrence Unique emergent macrophyte species recorded in each 1 m × 1 m quadrat replicate at each perpendicular transect in the amphibian zone 1 m × 1 m quadrat (n = 1 replicate per location) Categorical (species names)
Aquatic macrophyte occurrence Unique macrophyte species recorded from rake toss and dipnet sweep replicates within the aquatic zone Rake toss (n = 3) and dipnet sweep (n = 3) per location; species summed to determine total richness Categorical (species names)
Hydrology
Light penetration Depth at which the Secchi disc is no longer visible Secchi disc attached to marked line Continuous (cm)
Bottom visibility Visual assessment of whether the bottom was visible before physically disturbing the substrate Visual observation from shore Binary (0/1)
Total nitrogen (N) Dissolved total nitrogen in the water column 50 mL water sample, laboratory analysis Continuous (mg/L)
Total phosphorus (P) Dissolved phosphorus concentration in the water column 50 mL water sample, laboratory analysis Continuous (mg/L)
Electrical conductivity (EC) Conductivity of the water column 50 mL water sample, laboratory analysis Continuous (µS/cm)
